What we do

Collecting waste

Collecting waste refers to the process of gathering and removing waste materials from their sources, such as homes, businesses, and public areas. Waste can take many forms, including household garbage, recycling, hazardous waste, and construction debris. Waste collection is an essential service provided by local governments and private waste management companies. The goal of waste collection is to prevent the accumulation of waste in public areas, reduce the risk of environmental pollution and public health hazards, and promote sustainable waste management practices. Manufacture

Manufacturing is the process of producing goods on a large scale using machines, tools, and other equipment. It involves transforming raw materials or components into finished products that are suitable for use or sale. The manufacturing industry encompasses a wide range of sectors, including food and beverage, textiles, automotive, electronics, aerospace, and more. The manufacturing process typically involves several stages, including design, prototyping, production, and quality control. In the design phase, engineers and designers create detailed plans for the product, specifying its features, materials, and manufacturing process.

Shopping

Shopping for e-waste products can be a bit tricky as you want to ensure that the products you purchase are safe and reliable. Here are some tips which help you to purchase the products: Research the products you are interested in purchasing. Look for information about the product's safety and reliability. Look for products that have been certified by reputable organizations such as the Electronic Product Environmental Assessment Tool (EPEAT) or the Underwriters Laboratories (UL).
